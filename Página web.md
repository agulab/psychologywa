Con este texto:

Medicare

To access Medicare rebates for your sessions, you will need a referral and a specific structured care plan from your General Practitioner (GP). Depending on your diagnosis, your GP may issue a Mental Health Care Plan (MHCP), which covers up to 10 rebated individual sessions per calendar year, or an Eating Disorder Management Plan (EDMP), which provides access to up to 40 rebated psychological treatment sessions within a 12-month period. Our practice ethos is to keep quality mental health care accessible; therefore, we always aim to ensure your out-of-pocket (gap) expense does not exceed $100 AUD per session. If you do not hold a Medicare card or lack coverage through alternative third-party funding—such as an Employee Assistance Program (EAP) or private health insurance—we will match you with a qualified professional whose private fee structure aligns perfectly with your budget, ensuring you still only pay $100 AUD out of pocket.

Couples Therapy Fees

Please note that Couples Therapy is billed at a flat rate of $140 AUD per session. Unlike individual therapy, couples and family consultations are strictly private services in Australia. This means they are completely out-of-pocket expenses and cannot be subsidised by Medicare rebates, EAP funding, or other institutional third-party payees. 

 NDIS Funding and Support Types

For participants under the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS), we offer services aligned with your specific funding goals. Depending on your NDIS plan, you can request treatment funded under Psychosocial Recovery Coaching (focused on capacity-building and resilience for severe mental health conditions) or standard Capacity-Building Support (aimed at developing independence and daily living skills). Because NDIS pricing limits and regulations differ significantly based on the practitioner's credentials, we highly recommend checking your NDIS plan management type (Self-Managed, Plan-Managed, or NDIA-Managed). You must verify whether your specific plan allows you to engage a counsellor for these supports or if it strictly mandates the services of a registered psychologist, as hourly rates and internal clinic assignment protocols will vary accordingly.

Couples Therapy with Matias De Ambrosio: A Relational and Communication-Focused Approach

Matias De Ambrosio offers a distinctive and highly specialized approach to couples therapy, moving away from conventional methods. Rather than assigning standard homework tasks or behavioral exercises to perform at home, his practice shifts the focus entirely toward the live dynamic within the therapeutic session.

\#\#\#\# The Core Focus

The clinical interviews are structured around the immediate emotional and communicative reality of the couple. Matias focuses deeply on:

  Affective Exploration: Investigating what each member of the couple genuinely feels and expresses in the room.

  Communication Patterns: Deconstructing how the couple interacts and rebuilding a shared language.

  Conflict Resolution: Navigating disagreements, addressing underlying tensions, and fostering mutual understanding.

\#\#\#\# Areas of Clinical Specialization

Matias's practice is highly inclusive and adapted to contemporary relationship structures, providing safe, expert containment for a diverse range of clients:

  Diverse Couple Configurations: Extensive experience working with bilingual couples (navigating cultural and linguistic nuances), traditional couples, LGBTQ+ relationships, and neurodivergent couples (tailoring communication styles to varied cognitive processing profiles).

  Complex Family Dynamics: Deep clinical understanding of intricate systemic family structures and interpersonal conflicts.

Through this clinical framework, therapy becomes a profound space for insight and structural change, focusing on real-time resolution rather than prescriptive behavioral management.
